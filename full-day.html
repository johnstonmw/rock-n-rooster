An Immersive Escape Into the Ancient Past

With our Full-Day Experience, you’ll see all the iconic sights along Valley Drive that made Monument Valley a world-famous travel destination, and then go much farther. Leaving the graded road and crowds of tourists behind, we will explore massive sandstone arches — including Eye of the Sun, Ear of the Wind, and more — before pausing for a lunch in the welcome shade of the Big Hogan, a natural cave arch.

After a rest, we will drive to one of the more remote corners of the Navajo Tribal Park to visit some of the many hidden gems left untouched over centuries. Short walks from the comfort of our air-conditioned vehicle will lead to ancient Anasazi ruins tucked away and camouflaged in box canyons; we will examine midden piles that contain intricately decorated pottery shards and finely shaped arrowheads; admire elaborate petroglyph panels that stretch several feet high depicting hunting parties from a long-forgotten era; and spot rare wildlife — including great horned owls, wild stallions and reptiles — that miraculously survive in the unforgiving desert.

Travel in the comfort of an air-conditioned 4x4 with our experienced guides.

Evidence of the "Ancient Ones"

These rarely traveled trails and remote canyons are familiar territory to our founder and guide, Bennett Yazzie, who was raised herding sheep and cattle across the land. Countless hours on the range and exploring with family led Bennett to discover many of these ancient remnants of the “ancient ones,” as modern-day Navajos refer to the Anasazi who preceded them on the land a thousand years earlier.

Today, Bennett enjoys sharing the countless treasures of his homeland — from the towering, thousand-foot sandstone buttes to the smallest arrowhead uncovered in the sand — with guests from all over the world.
