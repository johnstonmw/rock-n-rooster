Limited Time? We'll Make the Most of It

Every holiday and road trip schedule is different and with so much to see in the American Southwest, we understand if your time visiting us in Monument Valley is limited. However, we promise to make the most of your short stay by guiding you to the most famous viewpoints and locations within the Monument Valley Tribal Park.

Based on your schedule, we will pick you up from the visitor center in the morning or at noon and make our way around the Valley Drive scenic loop in the comfort of an air-conditioned/heated 4x4 vehicle. Along the way, we will make all the mandatory stops, including the Mittens, Merrick Butte, John Ford’s Point, the Totem Pole and Yei Bi Chei, Sand Spring, Artist’s Point, the North Window, and much more.

Travel in the comfort of an air-conditioned 4x4 with our experienced guides.

Off the Beaten Path

We'll then take a detour and follow sandy washes and primitive roads to view four of the most spectacular sandstone arches in the park: Eye of the Sun, Ear of the Wind, Arch 3, and Arch 4.

One final stop will allow us to meet a local Navajo family who continue to practice traditional rug weaving, using wool sheared from sheep raised on the land. There, we will pause to learn more about the Navajo history and culture and have the opportunity to purchase handmade rugs and jewelry produced by the family. Less than a half hour later, we will return to the Visitor Center after a total of four hours of exploration within the Monument Valley Tribal Park.

We recommend that our guests pack at least a liter of water, sunscreen, a sunhat and snacks for the Half-Day Tour. Our guides will provide complimentary bottled water if you need it throughout the tour, but no food will be served on this trip. Please remember, all itineraries are flexible when you travel with Rock ’N’ Rooster Expeditions. Upon calling to book your tour with us, feel free to request any additional stops and we will do our best to accommodate you.
