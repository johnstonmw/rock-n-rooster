About Rock 'N' Rooster Expeditions

Monument Valley is best known for its iconic red landscape of towering sandstone buttes, made famous by Hollywood films that set their stories against this epic backdrop of geological giants. Located in the Four Corners area of the American Southwest, the Monument Valley Navajo Tribal Park now receives more than 250,000 visitors per year from all across the globe — many of whom spend an average of 2–3 hours enjoying the scenic Valley Drive.

However, the real beauty of Monument Valley extends far beyond the reaches of this heavily-trafficked tourist loop — through remote side canyons decorated by ancient human inhabitants, up winding, dusty roads that lead to abandoned mining camps, atop sandstone mesas set ablaze in the orange glow of sunset, and into the homes of the local Navajo residents, who graciously welcome their guests.

Founded by a desire to share these hidden treasures with guests from far and wide, Rock ’N’ Rooster Expeditions will take you deeper into this magical landscape and provide an intimate look at the history, geology, culture and tribal traditions of Monument Valley.

Your Host

Rock 'N' Rooster Expeditions was established upon the vision of Bennett Yazzie, a member of the Navajo Nation and resident of Monument Valley for more than 40 years. The grandson of a medicine man and son of a shepherd, Bennett spent his formative years learning the language and customs of his tribe, attending school in the town of Gouldings, and helping his family raise livestock on their land beneath Rooster Rock.

After leaving the reservation to attend college, Bennett eventually returned to Monument Valley — brought home by his desire to be among family and to be surrounded by the beauty the land where he was raised. A native Navajo speaker, a student of his tribe’s history and culture, and a kind and generous spirit, Bennett makes and exemplary guide to this land he knows and loves.

Enjoy the warm hospitality and local knowledge of your guide, Bennett Yazzie.
