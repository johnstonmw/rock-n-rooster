Sleep Beneath Monument Valley's Dazzling Night Sky

Are you the adventurous type? Do you often dream about exploring every fork in the road or trail you travel? If so, our Overnight Adventure is the ultimate Monument Valley tour itinerary for the free spirit in you. Over the course of 22 hours, we will take in everything this magical valley has to offer — from the jaw-dropping scenery to the daily life of its inhabitants.

Starting on Valley Drive, we will admire the legendary vistas that have made Monument Valley a familiar backdrop in countless Hollywood films — from old westerns to futuristic science fiction flicks. Then, leaving the tourist loop behind, we’ll explore sandy washes and rugged trails in our dependable and capable 4x4 to view the quieter corners of the Navajo Tribal Park.

First, we’ll make stops at towering sandstone arches — including Eye of the Sun and Ear of the Wind — and then pause for lunch in the shade of the Big Hogan. Following our meal, we will visit a Navajo family living nearby who still practice traditional rug weaving techniques.

The best Monument Valley tour for the adventurous visitor.

The People of Monument Valley, Past and Present

Onward, we’ll seek out Anasazi ruins hidden away in side canyons, where evidence of their ancient inhabitants — including pottery shards and arrowheads — is scattered across the desert floor. Beautiful pictographs and petroglyph panels that tretch several feet high adorn the canyon walls and, on occasion, the rare wildlife recorded in the Anasazi art work can be spotted along the way.

Late in the afternoon, our next stop will be our home for the night: a traditional Navajo hogan built directly beneath the iconic rock spires of the Totem Pole and Yei Bi Chei. After a short rest, we will explore nearby trails and take an evening drive to nearby Sand Springs, where the deep glow of sunset will set rippling sand dunes and sandstone towers ablaze.

Back at camp, we will enjoy a dinner of traditional Navajo fry bread (Navajo tacos) and settle in around the campfire as the dazzling night sky reveals itself. Depending on the moon phase and weather, we will be treated to either a brilliant view of the Milky Way, a bright moon illuminating the surrounding rock spires, or flashing thunderstorms over distant mountains.

Be more than just a tourist; experience life in Monument Valley.

Nearly 24 Hours of Exploration

The following morning, we will wake as the rising sun saturates the colors of the desert an even deeper orange. Breakfast will be served as we admire and photograph the beauty of our surroundings, and then we'll take our final scenic drive back to the the visitor center, topping off nearly a full 24-hour exploration of Monument Valley’s landscape and culture.

We recommend that our guests pack at least a liter of water, sunscreen, a sunhat and snacks for the Overnight Adventure. Our guides will provide complimentary bottled water throughout the tour, and lunch, dinner and breakfast on the second day will be served. Please inform us of any special dietary needs — such as food allergies, religious restrictions and vegetarian diets — at the time of booking. Cots, clean linen and blankets will be provided, but guests can also pack their own sleeping bags and personal comfort items for the overnight stay.

And remember, all itineraries are flexible when you travel with Rock ’N’ Rooster Expeditions. When you call to book your tour with us, feel free to request any additional stops and we will do our utmost best to accommodate you.
